Fell for Fall

I'm falling hard this fall,
But at least the view is pretty.
It's hard to stand tall,
When you're given looks of pity.

Yet, watching the leaves,
Softly descending upon my feet,
Makes it easier for me to believe,
There are still things left sweet.

I'd kick them freely into the air,
With a childlike smile upon my face,
As if I had time to spare,
Distracted from life's frantic race.

Just watching the colors fly,
Makes me giggle and cry,
And I could blame the cold,
But I'll embrace the panorama of gold.

The colors I once thought I'd lost,
Now flush red upon my cheeks,
And on the leaves of trees,
A glimpse of happiness, the wind speaks.

I wish every day could be like this,
But soon they'll all be bare,
And I'm scared I'll have to miss,
Saying goodbye to those I care.

Chasing the Moon That Chased Me

I always thought the moon followed me when I was little,
It was always there, staring down at me like a riddle.
So I'd stare back and smile with my eyes,
And let it lull me into soft lullabies.

It read me like a bedtime story,
And I wished to forever be its quarry.
Because I didn't want to say goodbye,
When it left me in the morning sky.
I'd always ask Dad why,
And he'd joke it'd be back when I'm snoring by.

I'd wish for the night to come so I could solve its riddle,
Though I knew when I try, I'd just be stuck in the middle.
And all I'd get is another little clue,
But now that I'm all grown up, I’m the one to follow the moon.

Perhaps one day I'll be able to solve it soon,
As I'm constantly chasing the skies in endless late-night drives.
Wishing the moon won't leave my side,
When it had always been there with me through my childhood ride.

Dreams of You

The moon would lay out the night,
With stars making a pretty sight,
Unintentionally leaving me sleepless.
Tossing and turning, my bed a mess.

If falling asleep were as easy as falling for you,
I’d close my eyes and dream of you.
I’d replay our awkward first-date smiles,
Butterflies fluttering in my stomach for miles.

Recalling every slight touch and witty exchange,
And how I also happened to make your stoic expression change.
It was amusing to see you all flustered,
By the cheesy lines I had mustered.

"I’d go again if it’s with you,"
When you asked if I had fun too.

But that was way back when,
I doubted we'd meet again.
All that remains are dreams of you,
And what-ifs that won't come true.
